HOW IT WORKS

Five steps. Every time. No shortcuts.

Here's exactly what happens, in order — from picking a target to getting your report back.

01

Pick a target

Know what you want explored — your own product, or a competitor's. The agent can't guess this part for you — come with the actual URL, not just a name.

02

You and the agent sign in together HUMAN-LED

You handle the real login yourself — filling in the email, the password, clicking through any verification — right in the shared browser window. The agent isn't watching yet, and has no access at all until the account genuinely exists.

WHY IT WORKS THIS WAY

Credentials never need to pass through the agent in the first place, rather than trusting the agent to handle them carefully. Nothing to leak, because there's nothing there to see.

03

You watch the agent explore on its own

Once you're signed in, the agent takes over — clicking through screens, reading labels, opening menus, mapping the product out as it goes. You're watching this happen live, in the same window, not reading about it afterward.

For every screen that's meaningfully different from the last, the agent records a structured snapshot: what the screen is for, how it's laid out, and every component the agent can identify. This is the raw material everything else is built from.

04

You can steer the agent, or it'll ask YOU DECIDE

See something you want the agent to focus on — a specific flow, a page you care about? Tell the agent directly, any time, and it'll factor that in right away.

The agent also knows when to check in on its own: if something is genuinely ambiguous, the agent stops and asks rather than guessing. And before anything with a real external effect — sending a message, connecting a real account, entering payment details, deleting anything — the agent always describes what it's about to do and waits for your OK. That last part isn't a setting you can turn off to move faster.

EXAMPLE

"This looks like it requires a connected email account before I can test sending — should I try connecting one, or leave this as an open question?"

05

You get your report

A structured screen inventory, a navigation flow map showing how everything connects, friction points tied to specific evidence rather than general impressions, and an honest account of anything the agent didn't get to — not a polished narrative dressed up to look more complete than the run actually was.
